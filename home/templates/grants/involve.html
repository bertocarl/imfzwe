Volunteering and Fundraising

Last Updated: March 19, 2019

INVOLVE (International Volunteer Venture), a grassroots volunteer group started by IMF staff in March 1994, is the main avenue for volunteerism in the IMF.

Staff members, their friends, families, and retirees participate in regularly scheduled projects which range from annual parties for children in need and monthly feeding programs for the homeless to rebuilding homes and mentoring children.

IMF staff volunteer thousands of hours a year, both in our host city and in locations around the world.

IMF Mentoring

IMF volunteers have been mentoring in local schools since 1996.

Our staff monthly helps over 50 students with reading, math and other subjects during lunch and after school, at Stevens Elementary School and Adams Elementary School.
 
Students and their mentors celebrate the end of the school year at Stevens Elementary School
  

IMF Summer Kids Camp

IMF Giving Together has sponsored a one-week summer day camp for children of low-income families at Bretton Woods Recreation Center in Germantown, Maryland.

During one week in August fifty children, aged 6 - 12 years enjoy swimming, tennis, soccer, nature hikes, games, and arts & crafts. The children come from the Latin American Youth Center and Calvary Bilingual Multicultural Learning Center/CentroNía.

IMF employees volunteered their time as counselors throughout the week.

Help the Homeless Walkathon

The IMF has been a major partner in Fannie Mae's annual Help the Homeless Walkathon since 2000, contributing at the Chairman's Circle level of $50,000.

In 2005, the walkathon raised over $7.8 million to help local nonprofits working with the homeless.

Staff and their families come out in full force for this event.
